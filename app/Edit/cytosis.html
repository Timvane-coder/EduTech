🧬 The Cell

Plasma Membrane: Phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins — the gatekeeper.

Cytoplasm: Aqueous medium where all transport events unfold.

Nucleus: Central organelle housing DNA.

⬇️ Endocytosis

Phagocytosis: Large particles (bacteria, debris) engulfed by membrane pseudopods.

Pinocytosis: Tiny droplets of extracellular fluid pulled in.

Receptor-Mediated: Specific ligands bind receptors → coated pit → internalization.

⬆️ Exocytosis

Constitutive: Continuous release of proteins (e.g. enzymes, hormones).

Regulated: Signal-triggered release (e.g. neurotransmitters, insulin).

Key step: SNARE proteins fuse vesicle membrane with plasma membrane.
