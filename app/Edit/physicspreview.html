Free Body Diagram

How it's built:
  • Block: A <rect> centred at (250,250) with a centre-of-mass crosshair dot.
  • Ground surface: A horizontal <line> with 16 diagonal hatch marks below, simulating a textbook surface indicator.
  • Force vectors: Each force is a <line> + <polygon> arrowhead in its own colour — green (N↑), red (W↓), blue (F→), amber (f←), purple (T at angle).
  • Tension angle: A dashed arc drawn with <path d="M…A…"> marks the angle θ between the tension vector and the horizontal.
  • Info box: A dashed <rect> with Newton's 2nd Law component equations and equilibrium condition.
  • Coordinate axes: Embedded mini x–y axes in the bottom-right corner for orientation.

Transverse vs Longitudinal Waves
transverseVsLongitudinalParticleMotionDiagramsvg
How it's built:
  • Transverse section: A cubic Bézier <path> approximates a sine wave (3.5 cycles). Amplitude (A) is marked with a double-headed red arrow, wavelength (λ) spans crest-to-crest.
  • Particle arrows (transverse): Small vertical red arrows at each crest/trough show displacement perpendicular (⊥) to propagation. Equilibrium nodes get a dot instead.
  • Longitudinal section: A bordered <rect> channel holds alternating compression bands (thick green lines, green-tinted <rect> highlight) and rarefaction gaps (sparse thin lines).
  • Particle arrows (longitudinal): Double-headed horizontal arrows (← →) in red below the channel show parallel (∥) motion.
  • C/R labels and λ bracket: Compression (C) and rarefaction (R) labels appear below each band; a bracketed λ arrow spans one C-to-C period.

Concave Mirror Ray Diagram
concaveMirrorRayDiagramFocalPointImageFormationDiagramsvg
How it's built:
  • Mirror arc: A quadratic Bézier <path d="M … Q … "/> creates the concave curve; a filled, slightly offset twin path adds the reflective body with hatch marks for the non-reflective back.
  • Principal axis: A horizontal line with tick marks and text labels for F and C (= 2F).
  • Object & image arrows: Upright blue arrow (object) and downward red arrow (image) built from <line> + <polygon> arrowheads. Image position derived analytically from mirror formula.
  • Ray 1 (red): Travels horizontally from object tip to mirror, then reflects through F and extends to the image.
  • Ray 2 (green): Travels from object tip toward F, hits mirror, then reflects parallel to the axis.
  • Ray 3 (purple): Travels from object tip through C and returns back — shown dashed after reflection to avoid clutter.
  • Formula boxes: Two dashed <rect> boxes summarise the mirror equation and a colour-coded ray legend.

Ammeter Series Circuit Diagram
ammeterSeriesConnectionCircuitDiagramsvg
How it's built:
  • Main circuit loop: Four straight <line> segments form a rectangular wire loop with gaps where components are inserted.
  • Battery (EMF ε): Two pairs of alternating long (positive) and short (negative) horizontal lines represent the cell stack, following the standard IEC circuit symbol convention.
  • Ammeter in series: A <circle> with an "A" label interrupts the top wire — the wire feeds in and out of the circle, enforcing the series topology. Labelled with its near-zero resistance property.
  • Resistor: A <polyline> zigzag (US/IEEE style) is embedded in the bottom wire segment, labelled R above.
  • Voltmeter in parallel: Dashed green lines branch from both sides of the resistor, loop below the circuit, and connect to a green circle "V" — visually distinct from the main loop, showing the parallel connection.
  • Current arrows: Red filled polygon arrowheads placed around the loop show conventional current direction (from + terminal).
  • Junction dots: Filled circles at wire junctions (including the voltmeter branch points in green) follow standard schematic notation.
  • Formula box: A dashed annotation panel lists I = ε/(R+r), V_R, power dissipation, and Kirchhoff's voltage law.
