01

Titration Apparatus — Labelled Setup

Standard acid-base titration: burette, stopcock, conical flask, analyte with phenolphthalein indicator, white tile, and retort stand — all annotated with leader lines.

Analytical Chem

Implementation Breakdown

  • Burette tube: A tall thin <rect> with light-blue fill representing the glass, overlaid with an orange-tinted <rect> for the NaOH solution level. A cubic bezier <path> approximates the meniscus curve at the solution surface.
  • Graduation marks: Alternating major (stroke-width 1.8) and minor (stroke-width 1) <line> elements spaced at 32 px intervals for 5 mL steps, with SVG <text> labels (0–50 mL).
  • Stopcock: An <ellipse> for the barrel, plus a rotated <rect> (transform="rotate(−30,…)") for the angled stopcock handle — matching real glassware.
  • Conical flask: A <polygon> trapezoid for the flask body, a narrower <rect> neck, and a second translucent <polygon> for the pink analyte solution. A cubic bezier swirl hints at mixing.
  • Titre drop: A small <ellipse> below the burette tip with a dashed <line> streak — indicating the half-drop technique at endpoint.
  • Leader-line labels: Every label uses a dashed <line> (stroke-dasharray="3,2") to point precisely to the described component — mirroring the physics diagram annotation style.

SVG primitives used

rect, polygon, path
ellipse, line, text
transform=rotate()
fill-opacity, stroke-dasharray
02

Atomic Structure — Electron Shell Configuration

Bohr model for Sodium (Z=11): three concentric dashed shells (K, L, M), electrons placed at geometrically correct positions, nucleus labelled with proton/neutron count, and a radial energy arrow.

Atomic Theory

Implementation Breakdown

  • Concentric shells: Three <circle> elements at radii 75, 145, 220 px, all centred at (280, 290), each with a distinct colour and stroke-dasharray="8,4" — a dashed line conventionally used for Bohr-model energy levels.
  • Nucleus: A filled <circle> (amber) with three <text> lines showing 11p⁺ / 12n⁰ / "nucleus" — keeping chemical notation compact within a small space.
  • Electron placement: K-shell electrons at 90° and 270° (top/bottom of r=75 circle). L-shell electrons at 45° intervals: positions computed as (cx + r·cosθ, cy − r·sinθ) and hard-coded as SVG coordinates. M-shell single valence electron at 45°, labelled distinctively.
  • Energy level arrow: A vertical <line> with arrowhead <polygon> on the left margin, with text rotated via transform="rotate(-90…)" — matching the convention from the physics wave diagram.
  • Configuration summary box: Dashed-border <rect> at base with both shell notation (2,8,1) and full orbital notation (1s²2s²2p⁶3s¹), bridging Bohr and quantum mechanical models.

03

Electrolysis — Anode/Cathode & Ion Migration

Electrolysis of CuSO₄(aq): beaker with electrolyte, two electrodes, DC battery in external circuit, Cu²⁺ and SO₄²⁻ migration arrows, electron flow direction, and half-equation boxes.

Electrochemistry

Implementation Breakdown

  • Beaker shape: Drawn with a rounded-corner <path> using L and Q (quadratic bezier) commands — the bottom corners are rounded (rx≈20) to simulate real glassware; a separate <path> fills the solution region.
  • Electrodes: Simple dark-grey <rect> bars extending from top of beaker into solution. The cathode has an orange copper-deposit <rect> layered on its inner face. The anode shows oxidation bubble circles (<circle> with no fill) rising from its surface.
  • Ion migration arrows: Bold solid <line> + <polygon> arrowheads in blue (Cu²⁺, leftward to cathode) and green (SO₄²⁻, rightward to anode). Secondary dashed arrows reinforce the directionality of multiple ionic species.
  • Battery symbol: Alternating thick <line> (short, negative plate) and thin <line> (tall, positive plate) pairs in the conventional electrochemical cell symbol, enclosed in a dashed bounding rect.
  • Electron flow: Purple <polygon> arrowheads on the external wire showing e⁻ direction (anode → power supply → cathode) — distinct from conventional current direction.
  • Half-equation boxes: Coloured <rect> panels (blue = cathode, red = anode) with the ionic half-equations, connected to the electrodes by dashed leader lines — directly mirroring the info-box convention from the physics diagrams.

Hydrocarbon Reaction Types — Cross-Reference Table

4×5 matrix showing Alkanes, Alkenes, Alkynes, and Arenes against Combustion, Addition, Substitution, Polymerisation, and Elimination — with ✓ / ~ / ✗ colour-coded cells and example equations.

Organic Chemistry

Implementation Breakdown

  • Grid structure: A 4-row × 5-column table built entirely from positioned <rect> elements. Each cell is an independent <rect> with a distinct fill and border colour — no HTML <table> used; pure SVG coordinate arithmetic (column x = 160 + col×106, row y = 125 + row×90).
  • Dark header row: Column headers use fill="#1e293b" (slate-900) with coloured reaction-type labels (orange=combustion, blue=addition, purple=substitution, green=polymerisation, pink=elimination) — each with a sub-label mini equation.
  • Three-tier colour coding: Green (#f0fdf4) for ✓, amber (#fefce8) for ~, red (#fff1f2) for ✗ — background fill + border stroke consistently applied. "KEY" cells get a thicker green border (stroke-width:2) to highlight the most characteristic reaction for each class.
  • Benzene ring in row header: The Arenes cell features a hand-drawn benzene ring: a <polygon> hexagon (6 points at 60° intervals) with an inner <circle> representing the delocalised π-system — a chemistry-specific visual shorthand.
  • Mini equations in cells: Multi-line <text> elements at font-size 8 provide example reactions, reagents, and conditions within each cell — matching the annotation density of the physics info-boxes.
  • Legend bar: A dashed-border footer <rect> with coloured swatches + text explaining the ✓/~/✗ encoding — a self-contained key mirroring the physics diagram legend boxes.
